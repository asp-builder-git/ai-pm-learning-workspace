
As a product builder, I want to build full stack applications to deliver needle moving business outcomes quickly. My primary building tools are VS Code + Claude, Kiro, Kiro CLI, Meshclaw (openclaw spin-off) among others. One of the ideas I had in mind was to build specialized agents for each of the skills that a product manager needs. These agents would be invoked automatically or manually in the building process to make it efficient for me to deliver solutions quickly. Here is a sample project (/Users/chiragg/Documents/kiro-projects/2026-projects/CORE-Estimator-Pilot). Here are the specialized skills I could spin off as agents. The idea would be that each of these hands off artefacts to the other minimizing the need for me to have back and forth conversations to make progress. 

1. Problem discoverer - monitors feedback channels, documents, business review artefacts to understand the biggest challenges faced by my customers. Critiques and pressure tests these for sound customer problem and business context understanding and maintains a healthy backlog
2. Opportunity or Entitlement Validator - critically analyzes the logic of the opportunity sizing as a senior financial analyst, validates it against other sources. Provides confidence score. Asks tough questions. Seeks further inputs when required. 
3. Current state expert - understands the current state of the tooling by analyzing the codebase. Understands architectural decisions taken and reasons for it. Understands the ecosystem of tooling within which the current tool sits. Keeps an eye on what other teams are building.
4. Solution generator - recommends multiple prototype solutions to solve problems. WOrks closely with the current state expert to assess the right fit. Is extremely enthusiastic and driven and often is full of ideas. 
5. Solution Assessor - Persona of a principal SDE and product manager rolled into one. Assesses solutions for value, feasibility, viability, usability. Provides detailed critique. understands the project need (bias vs quality) to recommend the right one
6. UX Designer - Senior UX Designer personal who understands the contemporary design practices, internal design language preference, empathizes deeply with the customer, pushes back to reduce complexity, ensures accessibility of design, and leverages components that make development and maintenance efficient. At Amazon, this could mean using Cloudscape AWS components as an example. 
7. UX Design lead - Reviews design delivery vs the brief provided (customer problem and proposed solution fit). Has a wide view of the prevalent tooling, the larger ecosystem, its challenges and provides critique and improvement ideas to drive design quality before final sign-off. 
8. Project Manager  persona who creates detailed project plans covering all stages of the product development process. Ensures transparency is provided to stakeholders. Plans meticulously. 
9. Go to market plan designer - Designs go to market artefacts to prepare for product launches across channels. Prepares for soft launches, UATs, multi channel communication plan. Sets up feedback collection mechanisms. 
10. Feedback & Insighs Manager who reads feedback provided by customers, gleans insights, feeds new ideas to backlog to ensure the product is being continually improved
11. Communications specialist  who excels at designing customer centric, easy to read copy for all the channels. Understands the ones customers prefer. Designs based on the need of the project, the stage, among other considerations. 

My vision is that I build a solution that eventually integrates with all the right channels, tools, and code base of my organization where the right agent autonomously executes its tasks, hands off to the next one, pings me for an input where needed and delivers solutions, improvements, features.
